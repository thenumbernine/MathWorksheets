$M =$ manifold.
$n =$ dimension of manifold.
$p \in M$ = point on manifold.
$T_p(M)$ = tangent space on $M$ at point $p$.
$T_p^*(M)$ = dual tangent space on $M$ at point $p$.

Let $\frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}$ be a derivative operator, and a vector basis on the tangent space of the manifold.
k-forms are functions mapping $\underbrace{T_p \otimes \dots \otimes T_p}_{\times k} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$.
Let $dx^a$ be the one-form basis dual to $\frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}$ basis
such that $dx^a(\frac{\partial}{\partial x^b}) = \delta^a_b$

Let $e_a = e_a(\frac{\partial}{\partial x^u})$ be a vector basis that spans $T_p(M)$.
$e_a : M \rightarrow V$ form a vector space.

Let ${e^a} = e^a(dx^u) = $ span $T_p^*(M)$ be the one-form basis dual to $e_a$.
$e^a : V \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, e^a$ linear
such that $e^a(e_b) = \delta^a_b$

If $e_a = \partial_a$ and $e^a = dx^a$ then the basis is called a coordinate basis / the basis is holonomic.

Tensors are objects invariant to change of basis.
Tensors exist within (p,q) tanganet and dual-tangent spaces:
$x = {x^{a_1 ... a_p}}_{b_1 ... b_q} e_{a_1} \otimes ... \otimes e_{a_p} \otimes e^{b_1} \otimes ... \otimes e^{b_q}$
Examples of tensors:
scalars: $\phi : M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$
rank-1 contravariant tensors (vectors): $v = v^a e_a$
rank-2 contravariant tensors: $v = v^{ab} e_a \otimes e_b$
rank-p contravariant tensors: $v = v^{a_1 ... a_p} e_{a_1} \otimes ... \otimes e_{a_p}$
rank-1 covariant tensors (one-form): $w = w_a e^a : V \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$
rank-2 covariant tensors: $w = w_{ab} e^a \otimes e^b : V \otimes V \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$
rank-p covariant tensors: $w = w_{a_1 ... a_p} e^{a_1} \otimes ... \otimes e^{a_p} : \underbrace{V \otimes ... \otimes V}_{\times p} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$

One-form applied to a vector:
For one-form $w \in V^*$ and vector $v \in V$
$w(v) = w (v^b e_b)$ by expanding $v$.
$= w v^b (e_b)$ by linearity of one-form functions.
$= v^b w_a e^a (e_b)$ by expanding $w$.
$= v^b w_a \delta^a_b$ by orthogonality of $e^a(e_b)$.
$= v^a w_a$

Outer product: maps (p,q) tensors and (r,s) tensors to (p+r,q+s) tensors
outer product of vectors: $ x \otimes y = x^a e_a \otimes y^b e_b = x^a y^b e_a \otimes e_b $
outer product of one-forms: $ x \otimes y = x_a e^a \otimes y_b e^b = x_a y_b e^a \otimes e^b $

Wedge product: $a \wedge b = a \otimes b - b \otimes a$
Wedge product antisymmetry: $a \wedge b = -b \wedge a$
If a rank-p covariant tensor has a completely antisymmetric basis then it is said to be a p-form.

Inner product: maps vectors and vectors to reals

Kronecher delta:
$\delta^a_b = 1$ for $a = b$, otherwise 0.
