wedge product of p-form basis: $ \boldsymbol{\omega}^{\alpha_1} \wedge \dots \wedge \boldsymbol{\omega}^{\alpha_p} = p! \cdot \boldsymbol{\omega}^{[\alpha_1} \otimes \dots \otimes \boldsymbol{\omega}^{\alpha_p]}$
wedge product of one-form basis: $ \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha \wedge \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta = \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha \otimes \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta - \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta \otimes \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha $
wedge product of one-forms: $ \textbf{a} \wedge \textbf{b} = a_\alpha \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha \wedge b_\beta \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta = a_\alpha b_\beta \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha \wedge \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta = a_\alpha b_\beta (\boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha \otimes \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta - \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta \otimes \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha) = (a_\alpha b_\beta - b_\alpha a_\beta) \boldsymbol{\omega}^\alpha \otimes \boldsymbol{\omega}^\beta = \textbf{a} \otimes \textbf{b} - \textbf{b} \otimes \textbf{a} $

wedge product basis antisymmetry: $ \textbf{dx}^\alpha \wedge \textbf{dx}^\beta = -\textbf{dx}^\beta \wedge \textbf{dx}^\alpha $

$ \textbf{a} \wedge \textbf{b} $

$ = a_{\alpha_1 \dots \alpha_p} b_{\beta_1 \dots \beta_q} (\textbf{dx}^{\alpha_1} \wedge \dots \wedge \textbf{dx}^{\alpha_p}) \wedge (\textbf{dx}^{\beta_1} \wedge \dots \wedge \textbf{dx}^{\beta_q}) $
