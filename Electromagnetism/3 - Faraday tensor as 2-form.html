Where $\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{E}_i dx^i$

Notice that $\mathcal{E}, \mathcal{B}$ are different than $E, B$.
$F_{uv} = \pmatrix{ 0 & -\frac{1}{c} \alpha \mathcal{E} \\ \frac{1}{c} \alpha \mathcal{E} & \alpha \star (\mathcal{B} \wedge dx^0) }$

So $F_{i0} = \frac{1}{c} \alpha \mathcal{E}_i$
And $F_{ij} = \epsilon_{ijk} B^k$

$\mathcal{E}_t$ and $\mathcal{B}_t$ can be chosen arbitrarily, since any values that are used will be cancelled.
