tetrads in coordinate basis

${e_a}$ is the tetrad, defined as ${e_a}^I \hat{x}_I$
${e_a}^I = \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^a}$

Let $e_a = \frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}$
So ${e_a}^I = \langle e_a, \partial x^I \rangle = \langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}, \partial x^I \rangle = \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^a}$

${e^a}_I$ is inverse tetrad, defined as
${e^a}_I = \frac{\partial x^a}{\partial x^I}$

Such that ${e^a}_I {e_b}^I = \frac{\partial x^a}{\partial x^I} \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^b} = \delta^a_b$
${e_a}^I {e^a}_J = \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^a} \frac{\partial x^a}{\partial x^J} = \delta^I_J$

coordinate tetrad derivatives

${{e_a}^I}_{,b} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x^b} \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^a}$
$= \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^b x^a}$
$= \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^a x^b}$
$= \frac{\partial}{\partial x^a} \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^b}$
$= {{e_b}^I}_{,a}$

Therefore, for coordinate tetrads, ${{e_{[a}}^I}_{,b]} = 0$

index ambiguity

Is ${e_a}^I = {e^I}_a$?

By definition of change of basis:
${e_a}^I = \frac{\partial x^I}{\partial x^a}$, while ${e^I}_a = ({e_I}^a)^{-1} = {e_a}^I$

By raising and lowering $a$'s by $g_{ab}$ and $I$'s by $\eta_{IJ}$
${e^I}_a = \eta^{IJ} {e_J}^b g_{ab}$
$= \eta^{IJ} {e^u}_J {e_u}^K {e^b}_K g_{ab}$
$= \eta^{IJ} \delta^K_J {e^b}_K g_{ab}$
$= \eta^{IK} {e^b}_K g_{ab}$
$= {e_a}^I$
So no, with $e$, the order of indexes doesn't matter, so long as top-vs-bottom is used.
However after raising/lowering it does make a difference. $e_{aI}$ and $e_{Ia}$ are different.

metric

$g$ is the metric defined as $g = g_{ab} dx^a dx^b = \langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}, \frac{\partial}{\partial x^b} \rangle dx^a dx^b = \langle e_a, e_b \rangle dx^a dx^b$
So $g_{ab}$ is defined as $g_{ab} = \langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}, \frac{\partial}{\partial x^b} \rangle = \langle e_a, e_b \rangle$

Let the inner product $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ as weighted by $\eta_{IJ}$
So $g_{ab} = {e_a}^I {e_b}^J \eta_{IJ}$

change of basis of metric

Let $g'_{a'b'} = \langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x^{a'}}, \frac{\partial}{\partial x^{b'}} \rangle$
$= \frac{\partial x^a}{\partial x^{a'}} \frac{\partial x^b}{\partial x^{b'}} \langle \frac{\partial}{\partial x^a}, \frac{\partial}{\partial x^b} \rangle$
$= {e^a}_{a'} {e^b}_{b'} g_{ab}$
$= g_{a'b'}$

Therefore $g$ = $g'$ regardless of change-of-basis.

change of basis of metric derivative

$g_{ab,c} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x^c} g_{ab}$
$g'_{a'b',c'} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x^{c'}} g'_{a'b'}$
$= (g'_{ab} {e_{a'}}^a {e_{b'}}^b)_{,c'}$
$= (g_{ab} {e_{a'}}^a {e_{b'}}^b)_{,c} {e_{c'}}^c$
$= (g_{ab,c} {e_{a'}}^a {e_{b'}}^b + g_{ab} ({{e_{a'}}^a}_{,c} {e_{b'}}^b + {e_{a'}}^a {{e_{b'}}^b}_{,c})) {e_{c'}}^c$
$= g_{ab,c} {e_{a'}}^a {e_{b'}}^b {e_{c'}}^c + g_{ab} ({{e_{a'}}^a}_{,c} {e_{b'}}^b + {e_{a'}}^a {{e_{b'}}^b}_{,c}) {e_{c'}}^c$
$= g_{ab,c} {e_{a'}}^a {e_{b'}}^b {e_{c'}}^c + g_{ab} ({{e_{a'}}^a}_{,c'} {e_{b'}}^b + {e_{a'}}^a {{e_{b'}}^b}_{,c'})$
$= g_{ab,c} {e_{a'}}^a {e_{b'}}^b {e_{c'}}^c + (g_{ab'} {{e_{c'}}^a}_{,a'} + g_{a'b} {{e_{c'}}^b}_{,b'})$

change of basis of connection

$\Gamma_{abc} = \frac{1}{2} (g_{ab,c} + g_{ac,b} - g_{bc,a})$
$\Gamma'_{a'b'c'} = \frac{1}{2} (g'_{a'b',c'} + g'_{a'c',b'} - g'_{b'c',a'})$
