...in flat space...

$D^i = \epsilon E^i + P^i$
$B^i = \mu H^i + M^i$

${D^i}_{,i} = \rho_{free}$ ... where $\rho_{free}$ = free charge (densitized?)
${B^i}_{,i} = 0$

${B^i}_{,t} = -\epsilon^{ijk} E_{k,j}$
${D^i}_{,t} = \epsilon^{ijk} H_{k,j} - J^i_{free}$

...substitute the time derivatives of $B^i$ and $D^i$ into definitions of $E^i$ and $H^i$
${E^i}_{,t} = \frac{1}{\epsilon} \epsilon^{ijk} H_{k,j} - \frac{1}{\epsilon} \epsilon_{,j} \frac{\partial x^j}{\partial t} E^i - \frac{1}{\epsilon} J^i_{free} - \frac{1}{\epsilon} {P^i}_{,j} \frac{\partial x^j}{\partial t}$
${H^i}_{,t} = -\frac{1}{\mu} \epsilon^{ijk} E_{k,j} - \frac{1}{\mu} \mu_{,j} \frac{\partial x^j}{\partial t} H^i - \frac{1}{\mu} {M^i}_{,j} \frac{\partial x^j}{\partial t}$
...assume $\frac{\partial x^i}{\partial t} = v^i$
${E^i}_{,t} = \frac{1}{\epsilon} \epsilon^{ijk} H_{k,j} - \frac{1}{\epsilon} \epsilon_{,j} v^j E^i - \frac{1}{\epsilon} J^i_{free} - \frac{1}{\epsilon} {P^i}_{,j} v^j$
${H^i}_{,t} = -\frac{1}{\mu} \epsilon^{ijk} E_{k,j} - \frac{1}{\mu} \mu_{,j} v^j H^i - \frac{1}{\mu} {M^i}_{,j} v^j$

The Drude model:
$(\rho v^i)_{,t} + (\rho v^i)_{,j} v^j = q (E^i + \frac{1}{\rho} \epsilon^{ijk} (\rho v_j) B_k) - \frac{1}{\tau} (\rho v^i)$
...here $\rho$ is the mass of the particles. I think it's safe to call it the density. $q$ is the electron charge. Is this densitized -- should it be scaled by $\rho$ as well?

The entire hyperbolic system, in matrix form:
$\left[\matrix{ B^i \\ D^i \\ H^i \\ E^i \\ \rho v^i }\right]_{,t} + \left[\matrix{ 0 & 0 & 0 & \epsilon^{ijk} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -\epsilon^{ijk} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \frac{1}{\mu} \epsilon^{ijk} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -\frac{1}{\epsilon} \epsilon^{ijk} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \delta^i_k v^j \\ }\right] \left[\matrix{ B^k \\ D^k \\ H^k \\ E^k \\ \rho v^k }\right]_{,j} = \left[\matrix{ 0 \\ -J^i_{free} \\ -\frac{1}{\mu} \mu_{,j} v^j H^i - \frac{1}{\mu} {M^i}_{,j} v^j \\ -\frac{1}{\epsilon} \epsilon_{,j} v^j E^i - \frac{1}{\epsilon} J^i_{free} - \frac{1}{\epsilon} {P^i}_{,j} v^j \\ q (E^i + \frac{1}{\rho} \epsilon^{ijk} (\rho v_j) B_k) - \frac{1}{\tau} (\rho v^i) \\ }\right]$
