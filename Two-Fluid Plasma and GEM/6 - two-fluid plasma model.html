two-fluid plasma model coupled with gravitoelectromagnetics
Going off of 2014 Abgrall, Kumar
and 2003 Loverich
with some extras from Wikipedia

constants:
$c =$ $\frac{m}{s} =$ speed of light.
$k_e = $ $\cdot \frac{kg \cdot m^3}{C^2 \cdot s^2}$ = Coulomb's constant (typically $\frac{1}{4 \pi \epsilon_0}$).
$\epsilon_0 = \frac{1}{4 \pi k_e} = $ {{vacuum_permittivity_in_C2_s2_per_kg_m3 = 1 / (4 * Math.PI * Coulomb_constant_in_kg_m3_per_C2_s2)}} $\cdot \frac{C^2 \cdot s^2}{kg \cdot m^3}$
$\mu_0 = \frac{1}{c^2 \cdot \epsilon_0} = $ {{vacuum_permeability_in_kg_m_per_C2 = 1 / (speed_of_light_in_m_per_s * speed_of_light_in_m_per_s * vacuum_permittivity_in_C2_s2_per_kg_m3)}} $\cdot \frac{kg \cdot m}{C^2} =$ vacuum permeability
such that $\frac{1}{\mu_0 \epsilon_0} = c^2$

material properties:
$\epsilon =$ material permittivity, in $\frac{C^2 \cdot s^2}{kg \cdot m^3}$
$\mu =$ material permeability, in $\frac{kg \cdot m}{C^2}$
$v_p = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu \epsilon} } =$ phase velocity through medium

electromagnetic properties:
$E =$ electric field, in $\frac{kg \cdot m}{C \cdot s^2}$
$D = \epsilon E =$ displacement field, in $\frac{C}{m^2}$
$B =$ magnetic field, in $\frac{kg}{C \cdot s}$
$H = \frac{1}{\mu} D = $ magnetizing field, in $\frac{C}{s}$

$\rho_{charge} = \epsilon \xi (\hat{\lambda}^2_d \hat{l}_r)^{-1} (r_{ion} \rho_{ion} + r_{elec} \rho_{elec}) =$ charge density, in $\frac{C}{m^3}$
$J = \epsilon ( \hat{\lambda}_d^2 \hat{l}_r )^{-1} (r_{ion} \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j + r_{elec} \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j) =$ charge current, in $\frac{C}{m \cdot s}$

GLM variables:
$\phi =$ electric divergence potential, in $\frac{C}{m^2}$
$\xi =$ speed of propagation of electric divergence, in $\frac{m}{s}$
$\psi =$ magnetic divergence potential, in $\frac{kg}{C \cdot s}$
$\kappa =$ speed of propagation of magnetic divergence, in $\frac{m}{s}$

GLM Maxwell equations:
$[\frac{C}{m^2 \cdot s}]: {D^j}_{,t} - \mu^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} {B^l}_{,j} + \xi \phi_{,j} = -J$
$[\frac{kg}{C \cdot s^2}]: {B^j}_{,t} + \epsilon^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} {D^l}_{,j} + \kappa \psi_{,j} = 0$
$[\frac{C}{m^2 \cdot s}]: \phi_{,t} + \xi \nabla_j D^j = c \rho_{charge}$ ... TODO I don't remember that 'c' being there, but how else would the units work out?
$[\frac{kg}{C \cdot s^2}]: \psi_{,t} + \kappa \nabla_j B^j = 0$

gravitoelectromagnetic equations:
${(D^g)^j}_{,t} - (\mu_g)^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} {(B^g)^l}_{,j} + \xi (\phi^g)_{,j} = -\frac{1}{\hat{c}} T_{0i}$
${(B^g)^j}_{,t} + (\epsilon_g)^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} {(D^g)^l}_{,j} + \kappa (\psi^g)_{,j} = 0$
$(\phi^g)_{,t} + \xi \nabla_j (D^g)^j = \epsilon_g \xi \frac{1}{\hat{c}} T_{00}$
$(\psi^g)_{,t} + \kappa \nabla_j (B^g)^j = 0$

fluid properties (for $\alpha = \{ ion, elec\}$):
$\rho_\alpha = $ ion and electron density, in $\frac{kg}{m^3}$
$v_\alpha = $ ion and electron velocity, in $\frac{m}{s}$
$P_\alpha = $ ion and electron pressure, in $\frac{kg}{m \cdot s^2}$
$\mathcal{E}_{\alpha,int} = \frac{P_\alpha}{\Gamma - 1} = $ densitized internal energy, related to the pressure by the ideal gas law, in $\frac{kg}{m \cdot s^2}$
for $\Gamma = \frac{5}{3} = $ the heat capacity ratio, in units $[1]$
$\mathcal{E}_{\alpha,kin} = \frac{1}{2} \rho_\alpha |v_\alpha|^2 = $ densitized kinetic energy, in $\frac{kg}{m \cdot s^2}$
$\mathcal{E}_{\alpha,total} = \mathcal{E}_{\alpha,int} + \mathcal{E}_{\alpha,kin} = $ densitized total energy, in $\frac{kg}{m \cdot s^2}$

$r_\alpha = \frac{q_\alpha}{m_\alpha}$ = charge/mass ratio
$m_\alpha = $ ion or electron mass, where in the simulation we set $m_{ion} = 1$

ions:
$[\frac{kg}{m^3 \cdot s}]: (\rho_{ion})_{,t} + \nabla_k (\rho_{ion} v_{ion}^k) = 0$
$[\frac{kg}{m^2 \cdot s^2}]: ( \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j )_{,t} + \nabla_k ( \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j v_{ion}^k + P_{ion} g^{jk}) = \rho_{ion} ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} (E^j + \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{ion})_k B_l) + ((E^g)^j + 4 \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{ion})_k (B^g)_l) )$
$[\frac{kg}{m \cdot s^3}]: ( \mathcal{E}_{ion,total} )_{,t} + \nabla_k (( E_{ion,total} + P_{ion}) v_{ion}^k) = \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} E_j + (E^g)^j )$

electrons:
$[\frac{kg}{m^3 \cdot s}]: (\rho_{elec})_{,t} + \nabla_k (\rho_{elec} v_{elec}^k ) = 0$
$[\frac{kg}{m^2 \cdot s^2}]: ( \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j )_{,t} + \nabla_k ( \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j v_{elec}^k + P_{elec} g^{jk}) = \rho_{elec} ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} (E^j + \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{elec})_k B_l) + ((E^g)^j + 4 \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{elec})_k (B^g)_l) )$
$[\frac{kg}{m \cdot s^3}]: ( E_{elec,total} )_{,t} + \nabla_k (( E_{elec,total} + P_{elec} ) v_{elec}^k ) = \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} E_j + (E^g)^j )$

$m = \frac{m^i}{m^e}$ = ion/electron mass ratio
$\hat{l}_r = \frac{l_r}{x_0} = \frac{W m_i v^T_{ion}}{q_{ion} B_0 x_0} =$ normalized ion Larmor radius
$\hat{c} = \frac{c}{v^T_{ion}}$ = normalized speed of light
$\hat{\lambda}_d = \frac{\lambda_d}{l_r} = \frac{1}{l_r} \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon (v^T_{ion})^2}{ n_0 q_{ion}}}$ = ion Debye length normalized with Larmor radius
$v^T_{ion} = $ reference thermal velocity
$B_0 = $ reference magnetic field
$x_0 = $ reference length

So, derived,
$L = \hat{\lambda}^2_d \hat{l}_r = \frac{\epsilon (v^T_{ion})^2 }{l_r x_0 n_0 q_{ion}}$
$= \frac{\epsilon B_0 v^T_{ion}}{W x_0 n_0 m_i}$

with terms added to 2014 Abgrall, Kumar:
$W = $ Lorentz boost, where 2014 Abgrall, Kumar sets $W = 1$
$\epsilon = $ material permittivity, assumed to be $\epsilon_0$ in the 2014 Abgrall Kumar paper

and stress-energy terms:
$T_{00} = c^2 \rho + \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{\epsilon} D^2 + \frac{1}{\mu} B^2) = $ energy from stress-energy
$T_{0i} = -c \rho v_i + \frac{1}{2 c} (n^2 + 1) S_i = $ momentum from stress-energy
