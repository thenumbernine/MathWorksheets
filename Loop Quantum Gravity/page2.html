   Summing over all even permutations of $\mu\nu\rho\sigma$ (such that $-\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}=1$) means every time the $\mu\nu\rho\sigma$ indexes are permuted an even number of times, so must the associated $a$'s be rearranged to match the original ${a^\alpha}_0 {a^\beta}_1 {a^\gamma}_2 {a^\delta}_3$. For this to happen the $\alpha\beta\gamma\delta$ indexes must be permuted an even number of times to match the original expression. Because $\alpha\beta\gamma\delta$ incides have been permuted an even number of times, the $\epsilon_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}$ doesn't change its sign.
   Likewise when summing the odd permutations of $\mu\nu\rho\sigma$, $-\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}=-1$, the $a$'s need to be rearranged to match the original expression, the $\alpha\beta\gamma\delta$ indexes have to be permuted an odd number of times, and $\epsilon_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}$ changes its sign as well to $-1$, so the term is each equal to the original expression.
