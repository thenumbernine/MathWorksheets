two-fluid plasma model coupled with gravitomagnetics
Going off of 2014 Abgrall, Kumar with some extras from Wikipedia

ions:
$(\rho_{ion})_{,t} + \nabla_k (\rho_{ion} v_{ion}^k) = 0$
$( \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j )_{,t} + \nabla_k ( \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j v_{ion}^k + P_{ion} g^{jk}) = \rho_{ion} ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} (E^j + \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{ion})_k B_l) + ((E^g)^j + 4 \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{ion})_k (B^g)_l) )$
$( E_{total,ion} )_{,t} + \nabla_k (( E_{total,ion} + P_{ion}) v_{ion}^k) = \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} E_j + (E^g)^j )$

electrons:
$(\rho_{elec})_{,t} + \nabla_k (\rho_{elec} v_{elec}^k ) = 0$
$( \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j )_{,t} + \nabla_k ( \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j v_{elec}^k + P_{elec} g^{jk}) = \rho_{elec} ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} (E^j + \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{elec})_k B_l) + ((E^g)^j + 4 \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{elec})_k (B^g)_l) )$
$( E_{total,elec} )_{,t} + \nabla_k (( E_{total,elec} + P_{elec} ) v_{elec}^k ) = \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j ( (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} E_j + (E^g)^j )$

GLM Maxwell equations:
${D^j}_{,t} - \mu^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} B^l + \xi \phi_{,j} = -\epsilon ( \hat{\lambda}_d^2 \hat{l}_r )^{-1} (r_{ion} \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j + r_{elec} \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j)$
${B^j}_{,t} + \epsilon^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} D^l + \kappa \psi_{,j} = 0$
$\phi_{,t} + \xi \nabla_j D^j = \epsilon \xi (\hat{\lambda}_d \hat{l}_r)^{-1} (r_{ion} \rho_{ion} + r_{elec} \rho_{elec})$
$\psi_{,t} + \kappa \nabla_j B^j = 0$

gravitomagnetic equations:
${(D^g)^j}_{,t} - (\mu_g)^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} (B^g)^l + \xi (\phi^g)_{,j} = -\frac{1}{\hat{c}} T_{0i}$
${(B^g)^j}_{,t} + (\epsilon_g)^{-1} \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} (D^g)^l + \kappa \psi_{,j} = 0$
$(\phi^g)_{,t} + \xi \nabla_j (D^g)^j = \epsilon_g \xi \frac{1}{\hat{c}} T_{00}$
$(\psi^g)_{,t} + \kappa \nabla_j (B^g)^j = 0$

(for $\alpha = \{ ion, elec\}$)
$\rho^\alpha = $ ion and electron density
$v^\alpha = $ ion and electron velocity
$P^\alpha = $ ion and electron pressure
$E^\alpha_{total} =$ ion and electron densitized total energy
(where $E^\alpha_{total} = \frac{1}{2} \rho^\alpha (v^\alpha)^2 + \rho E^\alpha_{int}$
and $E^\alpha_{int} = \frac{P^\alpha}{\gamma - 1}$ by the ideal gas law, with heat capacity ratio $\gamma$)
$r_\alpha = \frac{q_\alpha}{m_\alpha}$ = charge/mass ratio
$m_\alpha = $ ion or electron mass, where in the simulation we set $m_i = 1$
$m = \frac{m^i}{m^e}$ = ion/electron mass ratio
$\hat{l}_r = \frac{l_r}{x_0} = \frac{W m_i v_i^T}{q_i B_0 x_0} =$ normalized ion Larmor radius
$\hat{c} = \frac{c}{(v^i)^T}$ = normalized speed of light
$\hat{\lambda}_d = \frac{\lambda_d}{l_r} = \frac{1}{l_r} \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon (v_i^T)^2}{ n_0 q_i}}$ = ion Debye length normalized with Larmor radius
$v_i^T = $ reference thermal velocity
$B_0 = $ reference magnetic field
$x_0 = $ reference length
$E_{total,\alpha} = E_{int,\alpha} + E_{kin,\alpha} = $ densitized total energy
$E_{int,\alpha} = \frac{P_\alpha}{\gamma - 1} = $ densitized internal energy, related to the pressure by the ideal gas law
$E_{kin,\alpha} = \frac{1}{2} \rho_\alpha |v_\alpha|^2 = $ densitized kinetic energy
$\gamma = \frac{5}{3} = $ the heat capacity ratio

with terms added to 2014 Abgrall, Kumar:
$W = $ Lorentz boost, where 2014 Abgrall, Kumar sets $W = 1$
$\epsilon = $ material permittivity, assumed to be $\epsilon_0$ in the 2014 Abgrall Kumar paper

and stress-energy terms:
$T_{00} = c^2 \rho + \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{\epsilon} D^2 + \frac{1}{\mu} B^2) = $ energy from stress-energy (TODO double check units)
$T_{0i} = -c \rho v_i + \frac{1}{2 c} (n^2 + 1) S_i = $ momentum from stress-energy (TODO double-check units)
