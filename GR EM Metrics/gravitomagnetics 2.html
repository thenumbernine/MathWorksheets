$T_{ab} = \left[\matrix{ \rho_m c^2 & -\rho_m c v_i \\ -\rho_m c v_i & \rho_m v_i v_j + P \delta_{ij} }\right]$
So $T_{tt} = c^2 \rho_m$
(But the substitution of stress-energy for low-velocity matter isn't necessary. We can just as easily keep $T_{ab}$ in its original form.)
Combine with EFE to find:
$\Delta \bar{h}_{tt} = 16 \pi \frac{G}{c^4} T_{tt}$
$\frac{c^2}{4} \Delta \bar{h}_{tt} = 4 \pi \frac{G}{c^2} T_{tt}$
$\frac{c^2}{4} \Delta \bar{h}_{tt} = 4 \pi G \rho_m$

Poisson's equation:
$\frac{c^2}{4} \bar{h}_{tt} = -G \int_V \frac{\rho_m}{r} dV$
Let $\Phi^g = \frac{c^2}{4} \bar{h}_{tt}$
Let $G = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_g}$
$\Phi^g = -\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_g} \int_V \frac{\rho_m}{r} dV$
$\Phi^g = -\frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_g c^2} \int_V \frac{T_{tt}}{r} dV$
Tada! Just like electric field.

Next do the same trick with $T_{ti} = -\rho_m c^2 \frac{v_i}{c}$
$-\frac{c}{4} \bar{h}_{ti} = -\frac{G}{c^2} \int_V \frac{p_i}{r} dV$
Let $(A^g)_i = -\frac{c}{4} \bar{h}_{ti}$ be the gravitomagnetic potential vector.
Let $\mu_g = \frac{4 \pi G}{c^2}$.
$(A^g)_i = -\frac{\mu_g}{4 \pi} \int_V \frac{p_i}{r} dV$
$(A^g)_i = \frac{\mu_g}{4 \pi c} \int_V \frac{T_{ti}}{r} dV$
Just like the magnetic field.

This gives the familiar formula: $c^2 = \frac{1}{\epsilon_g \mu_g}$

Notice that $T_{ij}$ doesn't play into account at all.

two-fluid plasma model coupled with gravitomagnetics
Going off of 2014 Abgrall, Kumar with some extras from Wikipedia

ions:
$(\rho_{ion})_{,t} + \nabla_k (\rho_{ion} v_{ion}^k) = 0$
$( \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j )_{,t} + \nabla_k ( \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j v_{ion}^k + P_{ion} g^{jk}) = (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} \rho_{ion} (E^j + \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{ion})_k B_l) + \rho_{ion} ((E^g)^j + (B^g)^j)$
$( E_{total,ion} )_{,t} + \nabla_k (( E_{total,ion} + P_{ion}) v_{ion}^k) = (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} \rho_{ion} (E_j v_{ion}^j)$
electrons:
$(\rho_{elec})_{,t} + \nabla_k (\rho_{elec} v_{elec}^k ) = 0$
$( \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j )_{,t} + \nabla_k ( \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j v_{elec}^k + P_{elec} g^{jk}) = (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} \rho_{elec} (E^j + \epsilon^{jkl} (v_{elec})_k B_l) + \rho_{elec} ((E^g)^j + (B^g)^j)$
$( E_{total,elec} )_{,t} + \nabla_k (( E_{total,elec} + P_{elec} ) v_{elec}^k ) = (\hat{l}_r)^{-1} \rho_{elec} (E_j v_{elec}^j)$
GLM Maxwell equations:
${E^j}_{,t} - \hat{c}^2 \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} B^l + \xi \hat{c}^2 \phi_{,j} = -( \hat{\lambda}_d^2 \hat{l}_r )^{-1} (r_{ion} \rho_{ion} v_{ion}^j + r_{elec} \rho_{elec} v_{elec}^j)$
$(B^j)_{,t} + \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} E^l + \kappa \psi_{,j} = 0$
$\phi_{,t} + \xi \nabla_j E^j = \xi (\hat{\lambda}_d \hat{l}_r)^{-1} (r_{ion} \rho_{ion} + r_{elec} \rho_{elec})$
$\psi_{,t} + \kappa \hat{c}^2 \nabla_j B^j = 0$
gravitomagnetic equations:
${(E^g)^j}_{,t} - \hat{c}^2 \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} (B^g)^l + \xi \hat{c}^2 (\phi^g)_{,j} = -\hat{c} \mu^g T_{ti}$
$((B^g)^j)_{,t} + \nabla_k \epsilon^{jkl} (E^g)^l + \kappa \psi_{,j} = 0$
$(\phi^g)_{,t} + \xi \nabla_j (E^g)^j = \xi \frac{1}{\epsilon_g \hat{c}^2} T_{tt}$
$(\psi^g)_{,t} + \kappa \hat{c}^2 \nabla_j (B^g)^j = 0$

(for $\alpha = \{ ion, elec\}$)
$\rho^\alpha = $ ion and electron density
$v^\alpha = $ ion and electron velocity
$P^\alpha = $ ion and electron pressure
$E^\alpha_{total} =$ ion and electron densitized total energy
(where $E^\alpha_{total} = \frac{1}{2} \rho^\alpha (v^\alpha)^2 + \rho E^\alpha_{int}$
and $E^\alpha_{int} = \frac{P^\alpha}{\gamma - 1}$ by the ideal gas law, with heat capacity ratio $\gamma$)
$r_\alpha = \frac{q_\alpha}{m_\alpha}$ = charge/mass ratio
$m_\alpha = $ ion or electron mass, where in the simulation we set $m_i = 1$
$m = \frac{m^i}{m^e}$ = ion/electron mass ratio
$\hat{l}_r = \frac{l_r}{x_0} = \frac{W m_i v_i^T}{q_i B_0 x_0} =$ normalized ion Larmor radius
$\hat{c} = \frac{c}{(v^i)^T}$ = normalized speed of light
$\hat{\lambda}_d = \frac{\lambda_d}{l_r} = \frac{1}{l_r} \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon (v_i^T)^2}{ n_0 q_i}}$ = ion Debye length normalized with Larmor radius
$v_i^T = $ reference thermal velocity
$B_0 = $ reference magnetic field
$x_0 = $ reference length
$E_{total,\alpha} = E_{int,\alpha} + E_{kin,\alpha} = $ densitized total energy
$E_{int,\alpha} = \frac{P_\alpha}{\gamma - 1} = $ densitized internal energy, related to the pressure by the ideal gas law
$E_{kin,\alpha} = \frac{1}{2} \rho_\alpha |v_\alpha|^2 = $ densitized kinetic energy
$\gamma = \frac{5}{3} = $ the heat capacity ratio

with terms added to 2014 Abgrall, Kumar:
$W = $ Lorentz boost, where 2014 Abgrall, Kumar sets $W = 1$
$\epsilon = $ material permittivity, assumed to be $\epsilon_0$ in the 2014 Abgrall Kumar paper

and stress-energy terms:
$T_{tt} = \rho + \frac{1}{2} (E^2 + B^2) = $ energy from stress-energy (TODO double check units)
$T_{ti} = \rho v_i + S_i = $ momentum from stress-energy (TODO double-check units)
