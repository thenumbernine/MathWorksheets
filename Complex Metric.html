This means I have to become more formal when raising/lowering: whether it involves a left or a right multiplication of the metric?
For example, consider $E_i$
For left multiply, $E_i = \gamma_{ij} E^j = (\tilde{\gamma}_{ij} - i \tilde{\epsilon}_{ijk} B^k) E^j$
For right multiply, $E_i = E^j \gamma_{ji} = \bar{\gamma}_{ij} E^j = (\tilde{\gamma}_{ij} + i \tilde{\epsilon}_{ijk} B^k) E^j$
So left-multiply lowering vectors and right-multiply lowering vectors produce conjugates of one another.
If a vector is lowered with left-multiplication, it must be raised with left-multiplication of the inverse as well -- or right-multiplication of the inverse conjugate.
